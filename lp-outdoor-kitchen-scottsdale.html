Common Questions

Outdoor Kitchen FAQ

How much does a custom outdoor kitchen cost in Scottsdale?

Custom outdoor kitchens in Scottsdale typically range from $15,000 for a simple BBQ island with countertop to $80,000+ for full kitchens with pergola, pizza oven, bar, appliances, and lighting. The most common scope — a full-featured island with grill, side burners, refrigerator, and granite countertop — runs $20,000–$40,000. We provide free itemized quotes so you know exactly where every dollar goes.

Do outdoor kitchens require permits in Scottsdale?

Generally yes — any outdoor structure with gas, electrical, or plumbing connections requires permits from the City of Scottsdale or Maricopa County. We handle the full permit process as part of our build, including submitting drawings, coordinating inspections, and getting sign-off. You don't need to manage any of that.

What materials hold up best in Arizona heat?

For countertops, granite and porcelain tile are the most heat-resistant and durable in the Arizona climate. For islands, CMU (concrete masonry unit) block with stucco or stone veneer outperforms wood framing in the long run. We specifically avoid products that warp, fade, or degrade in extreme UV — and we'll walk you through material options during your consultation.

How long does an outdoor kitchen build take?

A standard outdoor kitchen build typically takes 2–4 weeks from permit approval to completion. Simpler BBQ island projects can be done in 1–2 weeks. More complex builds involving significant masonry, multiple trades, and permitting may run 4–6 weeks. We give you a detailed timeline with your quote so you can plan around your calendar.

Can you integrate the kitchen with our existing patio and pool area?

Absolutely — in fact, that's how most of our projects work. We design the outdoor kitchen as part of the larger backyard environment, coordinating with existing pool coping, paver patterns, shade structures, and landscaping. Our design process starts with a full site evaluation so the final result feels integrated, not bolted on.
