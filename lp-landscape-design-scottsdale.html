Landscape Design FAQ

How much does a full backyard remodel cost in Scottsdale?

Full backyard remodels in Scottsdale typically range from $40,000 to $300,000+ depending on scope, lot size, and finishes. A mid-tier transformation — turf, travertine patio, BBQ island, and landscaping — often runs $60,000–$120,000. Luxury estates with outdoor kitchens, putting greens, pergolas, and extensive hardscape can reach $200,000+. We work across all these ranges and design to your budget, not beyond it.

Is the 3D design included in the cost?

The initial design consultation and conceptual sketch are always free. For full 3D renderings and detailed design work, we include this as part of the build contract — meaning there's no separate design fee if we proceed with construction. If you want detailed renderings without committing to a build, we can discuss a standalone design fee.

How long does a full backyard remodel take?

Project timelines vary by scope. A focused project — patio plus turf, for example — can be completed in 1–2 weeks. Full backyard transformations involving multiple trades, permits, and complex construction typically take 4–12 weeks. We build a project schedule with milestones into every proposal so you know exactly when each phase starts and finishes.

Do you work in gated communities like Silverleaf and DC Ranch?

Yes — we work in Silverleaf, DC Ranch, Desert Mountain, Troon North, Gainey Ranch, and most gated communities throughout Scottsdale and Paradise Valley regularly. We're familiar with HOA submittal processes, guard gate protocols, and community architectural standards in these neighborhoods. We've handled dozens of projects in these communities and know how to manage the approval process efficiently.

Do you handle permits?

Yes — permit pulling is included in our project management for all work that requires it. This includes structural shade structures, outdoor kitchens with gas and electrical, and any grading or drainage work requiring city review. We submit drawings, track approval status, schedule inspections, and get final sign-off. You don't manage any of this.
