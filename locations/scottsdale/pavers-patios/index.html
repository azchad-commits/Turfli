Travertine

Our Installation Process

A properly installed paver surface starts long before the first stone is set.

1

Site Assessment & Design

We evaluate drainage, soil type, load requirements, and existing structures. We present 2–3 material and layout options with photo renders before any work begins.

2

Permitting & HOA Approval

We handle Scottsdale permit applications and work within your HOA guidelines — including Silverleaf's DMCA and DC Ranch's DRC processes — so you don't have to.

3

Excavation & Base Prep

Proper depth excavation (4–6" for patios, 8–12" for driveways), compacted gravel base, and sand bedding layer — the invisible work that determines how long your pavers last.

4

Paver Installation & Cutting

Precise layout, wet-saw cutting around obstacles, and tight joint tolerances. We don't rush — pattern alignment and level transitions are checked continuously.

5

Sealing & Final Inspection

Polymeric sand jointing to lock units in place, followed by a penetrating sealer application. Walk-through with you before we consider the project complete.

Pavers & Patios — FAQ

What's the best paver material for a Scottsdale pool deck?

Travertine is our most popular pool deck material in Scottsdale. Its natural porous structure keeps surface temperatures 20–25°F cooler than concrete, it's naturally slip-resistant when wet, and it never fades in UV. Porcelain pavers are a close second for a more contemporary look — they're non-porous and virtually maintenance-free.

How long do paver installations take?

Most residential patio projects take 3–7 days depending on size and complexity. Pool deck replacements typically run 5–10 days. Driveway installations range from 3–8 days. We'll give you a timeline before starting and keep you updated throughout.

Do pavers require a Scottsdale building permit?

In most cases, paver patios under a certain square footage don't require permits, but driveways and pool deck work often do. We handle the permit process as part of every project — you won't need to navigate Scottsdale's Development Services Department on your own.

How often do pavers need to be resealed in Arizona?

Arizona's intense UV and heat accelerate sealer breakdown compared to cooler climates. We recommend resealing every 2–3 years for concrete pavers and every 3–4 years for travertine. We offer annual maintenance programs that include inspection, joint sand replenishment, and resealing.

Can you repair sections of existing paver work?

Absolutely. One of the advantages of pavers over poured concrete is that individual units can be removed and replaced without disturbing the entire surface. We handle paver repairs, releveling, and re-sanding for both our own work and pavers installed by others.

What does a paver patio cost in Scottsdale?

Installed cost varies by material and project complexity. Concrete pavers typically run $18–$28/sq ft installed; travertine $22–$35/sq ft; flagstone $25–$40/sq ft; porcelain $30–$50/sq ft. Contact us for a precise estimate — we provide detailed line-item quotes at no charge.
