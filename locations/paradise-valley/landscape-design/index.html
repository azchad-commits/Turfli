Designing for Paradise Valley's Extraordinary Standard

Paradise Valley isn't just wealthy — it's intentional. The town's strict building standards, architectural review processes, and lot minimums exist to protect a quality of environment found nowhere else in metropolitan Phoenix. Landscape design here must match that standard.

Our principal designers bring a background in architecture and horticulture — not just landscaping. Every PV project begins with a full site analysis before a single plant or paver is specified.

Paradise Valley Permits — We Handle Everything

The Town of Paradise Valley has one of the most thorough review processes in the Valley. Landscape work that breaks ground, disturbs native plants, affects drainage, or involves structures requires review by PV's Planning & Zoning department. Projects exceeding certain thresholds require a full landscape plan stamped by a licensed landscape architect.

Turfli's design team includes licensed landscape architects with established relationships at PV's Development Services. We've successfully permitted projects at virtually every lot type in the town — hillside, wash-adjacent, and view corridor lots included. You won't navigate this alone.

Our Design Philosophy for Paradise Valley

🌵

Desert First

The Sonoran Desert is one of the world's most biodiverse ecosystems. We design with it, not against it — using native species, preserving existing desert plants, and letting the land inform the design.

🏠

Architecture Integration

Great landscape design is an extension of the architecture, not a separate layer placed around it. We study your home's lines, materials, and proportions before placing anything.

💧

Water Stewardship

Paradise Valley's water future is a real concern. We design water-efficient systems — drip irrigation, rainwater harvesting, grey water reuse — that maintain beauty without waste.

PV Landscape Design — FAQ

What's the minimum project size you take in Paradise Valley?

We focus on comprehensive estate projects rather than small-scope work. Most PV landscape design projects start at $75,000 in installation value. We're not the right fit for a single garden bed refresh, but for complete property transformations — or phased master plans — we're well positioned to help.

Do all landscape projects in Paradise Valley need permits?

Not all, but many do. The Town of PV requires permits for grading and drainage work, construction of structures (walls, pergolas, ramadas), new pool or water feature installation, and removal of native plants above certain trunk diameters. We assess permit requirements for every project and handle all submissions as part of our standard service.

Can you work alongside our architect and interior designer?

Absolutely — this is how the best PV projects are done. We welcome early coordination with your architect, pool designer, contractor, and interior designer. We'll attend design meetings, share CAD files, and coordinate scheduling to ensure the outdoor environment integrates seamlessly with the construction sequence.

How do you handle native plant preservation requirements?

The Town of PV and Maricopa County have native plant ordinances that require permits to remove, transplant, or impact saguaros and other protected species. We inventory all native plants before design begins, assess which can be preserved in-place versus transplanted, and obtain all required Native Plant Permits from Maricopa County.

What does a complete PV landscape design project typically cost?

Estate landscape design projects in Paradise Valley typically range from $120,000 to $600,000+ depending on property size, scope, and material selections. Design fees are typically 10–15% of the installation value and are credited toward installation if we're selected to build. We provide a detailed scope-of-work proposal before any work begins.
