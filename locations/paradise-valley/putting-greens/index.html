Putting Green FAQs — Paradise Valley

Common Questions

How realistic is a backyard putting green in terms of ball roll and break?

With the right product and installation, very realistic. Turfli uses nylon putting turf specifically designed for pace and ball roll consistency. The key is grade precision — we map contours to create natural-feeling breaks without extreme or unplayable slopes. Most clients tell us it actually improves their green reading because they can study breaks repetitively.

How much space is needed for a meaningful putting green in PV?

A useful single-hole practice green starts at around 200 sq ft. For a genuinely enjoyable multi-hole experience, 500–1,500 sq ft is ideal. Most Paradise Valley lots have ample space — we work with whatever you have.

Does a putting green add value to a Paradise Valley home?

Yes — consistently. In the Paradise Valley and North Scottsdale luxury market, a quality putting green is a recognized premium feature that buyers expect and value. A well-built green typically adds more than its cost in perceived value.

How do you handle the PV building permit process for putting greens?

Putting greens typically don't require permits as they're considered landscaping rather than structures. However, if we're adding lighting, electrical, or drainage that connects to the main system, those elements may require permits — which we pull and manage.
