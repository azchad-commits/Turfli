Paver Patio FAQ

How much does a paver patio cost in Scottsdale?

Paver patio costs in Scottsdale range from $15–$45 per square foot installed, depending on the material, base requirements, and complexity of the pattern. A 500 sq ft concrete paver patio typically runs $8,000–$15,000. Travertine pool decks and natural stone installations are at the higher end. We provide free, itemized quotes with no hidden charges.

How do travertine pavers hold up in Arizona heat?

Travertine is one of the best paver materials for Arizona because it naturally stays cooler than concrete or clay brick in direct sun. It's also non-slip when tumbled or brushed. The key is proper sealing — we seal every travertine installation to prevent staining and protect the stone from the Arizona UV environment.

Can you demo my existing concrete slab before installing pavers?

Yes — full demolition and haul-off is included in most paver quotes. We break up existing concrete, remove it from your property, prep the sub-grade, install proper base material, and then lay the pavers. We can also overlay pavers on an existing slab in certain situations — we'll evaluate and recommend the right approach during your site visit.

How long does paver installation take?

Most residential paver patios take 2–5 days from demo to cleanup. Smaller patios (under 400 sq ft) can be completed in 2 days. Large pool decks, driveways, or projects involving significant demolition may take 1–2 weeks. We'll give you a realistic timeline with your quote.

Do pavers need to be re-sealed or maintained?

Travertine and natural stone should be re-sealed every 2–4 years depending on sun exposure and foot traffic. Concrete pavers benefit from sealing every 3–5 years. We offer a maintenance program for existing clients. Day-to-day maintenance is minimal — occasional sweeping and hosing is typically all that's needed.
