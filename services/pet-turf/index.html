Pet Turf FAQ

Does artificial turf actually hold up to large, active dogs?

Yes, when the right product is selected. We use higher face-weight turf (typically 70–90 oz) with reinforced backing for dog runs and heavy-use pet areas. These products are rated for commercial pet applications and hold up significantly better than residential-grade turf under constant heavy use.

Will pet turf smell in the Arizona heat?

Not if it's installed correctly. The most common cause of pet turf odor is inadequate drainage combined with an infill that traps odor compounds. Our installs use ZeoFill or antimicrobial infill and a proper flow-through drainage system — liquids don't pool, bacteria don't build up, and the surface stays fresh even in 115°F summer heat.

Is artificial turf safe if my dog chews on it?

The turf products we install are made from non-toxic polyethylene and polypropylene fibers — no harmful chemicals, no heavy metals. While we'd prefer your dog not chew the turf, the materials are not dangerous if small amounts are ingested. We can also install chew-deterrent border systems to protect the turf edges.

How do I clean pet turf?

Remove solid waste as you would from a regular lawn. For liquid, the drainage system handles it automatically. A weekly rinse with a garden hose keeps the surface clean and fresh. Monthly or quarterly enzyme treatment is recommended to neutralize any residual odor compounds. Annual professional grooming and infill refresh helps maintain long-term performance.
