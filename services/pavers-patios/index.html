Why Base Work Matters

Pavers That Stay Beautiful — Not Just Look Beautiful

Pavers that shift, crack, or sink after a few years almost always have one root cause: bad base preparation. Insufficient depth, poor compaction, inadequate edge restraints, or no provision for monsoon drainage.

Turfli uses engineered base depths matched to soil conditions, mechanically compacted sub-base layers, premium polymeric sand jointing, and positive drainage design on every project.

Common Questions

Pavers & Patios FAQ

How much does paver installation cost in Scottsdale?

Installed paver patios in Scottsdale typically run $15–$45 per square foot depending on material, base requirements, and pattern complexity. A 500 sq ft concrete paver patio averages $8,000–$15,000. Travertine pool decks and natural stone are at the higher end. We provide free itemized quotes.

How do travertine pavers hold up in Arizona heat?

Travertine is one of the best pool deck and patio materials for Arizona specifically because it stays cooler than concrete in direct sun, resists UV fading, and provides natural slip resistance. Proper sealing extends its life significantly in our climate.

Can you demo existing concrete before installing pavers?

Yes — demo and haul-off is included in most paver quotes. We break up the existing slab, remove material, prep the sub-grade, install base, and lay pavers. In some cases we can overlay pavers on a stable existing slab — we'll evaluate at your site visit.

How long does paver installation take?

Most residential patios take 2–5 days. Simple 300–400 sq ft patios can be done in 2 days. Large pool decks or driveways may take 1–2 weeks. We'll give you a realistic timeline with your quote.
