Questions

Pergola FAQ

Do pergolas require permits in Scottsdale?

Yes — most freestanding and attached pergolas require permits in Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, and surrounding municipalities. Requirements depend on structure height, footprint, attachment method, and whether electrical or fans are integrated. We handle all permitting as part of the project.

How much does a pergola cost in Scottsdale?

Alumawood pergola installations in Scottsdale typically range from $8,000 to $25,000 depending on size, complexity, and add-ons like fans, lighting, or privacy screens. Steel structures and custom natural wood pergolas are generally at the higher end. We provide free itemized quotes.

What's the best pergola material for Arizona?

Alumawood is the most popular and practical choice for Arizona — it looks like wood but requires zero maintenance, never rots, and stands up to UV and temperature extremes indefinitely. Steel is better for large spans and modern aesthetics. Natural wood is beautiful but requires periodic sealing and maintenance in our climate.

Can you add fans, lighting, and misters to a pergola?

Yes — we can integrate ceiling fans, LED lighting, and misting systems into most pergola installations. Electrical runs are planned at the design phase and installed by a licensed electrician coordinated through us. Misting systems can also be added as a separate project to existing structures.
